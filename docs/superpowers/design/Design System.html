BootCamp · Design System v0.1

A design system for deep technical learning.

Dark-mode first. Editorial typography paired with IDE precision. The peacock palette gives every screen a sense of depth — iridescent teal-to-magenta accents on calm graphite surfaces, so code, lessons, and progress all sit in the same visual world.

Open the app Browse tokens
Principle 01
Calm depth

Surfaces are quiet. Color earns its place — used to signal track, progress, and mastery, never decoration.

Principle 02
Code-native

The platform is for developers. Mono fonts, monospaced numerics, and IDE-style frames belong in the everyday UI — not just lessons.

Principle 03
One step at a time

Lessons are short. Feedback is immediate. The system rewards small wins with subtle motion and tactile micro-interactions.

Voice & tone

Do
  • Speak directly. "You'll write a function that…"
  • Name the concept once, use it consistently after.
  • Celebrate small wins with restraint. "Nice — that compiles."
  • Use code in prose: let, not "the keyword let".
Don't
  • Be overly congratulatory. "Amazing job, superstar!" is a no.
  • Hide errors. Show what failed and why.
  • Use emoji as punctuation. The brand has its own visual vocabulary.
  • Mix metaphors — "Level up your coding journey" is two clichés.
